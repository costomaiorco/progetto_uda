Comparison of E-Waste Recycling Methods

The three e-waste recycling methods discussed in the previous pages—mechanical shredding, chemical leaching, and manual component extraction—each offer distinct advantages and disadvantages depending on the goal of the process. Mechanical shredding is fast and scalable but less precise in material separation, chemical leaching allows high recovery rates of specific valuable metals but raises environmental concerns due to the use of hazardous substances, while manual extraction provides the highest level of precision and material quality recovery but is labor-intensive and costly.
